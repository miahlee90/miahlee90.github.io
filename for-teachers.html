For Instructors

How Mia Music Studio works, how your students use it, and how you'll manage your class — in a few minutes.

What it is

One shareable site — miamusicstudio.com — that branches into four hands-on labs: 🎵 Theory Lab (107 interactive lessons), 🎹 Piano Lab (technique with a MIDI or on-screen keyboard), 🎧 Aural Lab (ear training), and a 🎮 Game Lab for fun. Everything runs in a browser on any computer, tablet, or phone — nothing to install.

1Try it yourself right now — no sign-in needed

Open the hub, pick a lab, and start a lesson. You can do everything a student can, without any account:

① LearnWatch & listen. Play the whole exercise or step through it note by note.
② PracticePress Start and play the highlighted notes — the app waits for you, no pressure.
③ Test / QuizPlay in tempo (or take a fresh-question quiz). Your score is recorded.

Students can skip around and repeat anything. Only the final Test/Quiz counts toward a grade. Best with a USB MIDI piano in Chrome or Edge — no keyboard? Tap the on-screen keys.

2How students use it

Without signing in — anyone can open a lab and learn. Their progress is saved on that one device, and nothing is reported. Great for trying it out or a quick classroom activity.

Signed in with your class codes coming when set up — a student signs in once and it works across every lab. Their completed lessons and Test/Quiz scores are saved to their account and reported to you automatically, from any device.

Note: students can never create their own account. They can only sign in with the Class Code + Access Code you give them — so no strangers can register. Anyone can still use the labs anonymously without any login.

3Managing your class once online sign-in is enabled

This is the flow once we turn on the class system (a one-time setup). It's the same across all labs — one approved email, one class, one student login for everything.

  1. Sign in — open any lab → Instructor sign-in with your approved email (a one-time link is emailed to you).
  2. Create a class — name it (e.g. "Class Piano I — Tue"). You get a Class Code.
  3. Add your students — each student gets a personal Access Code. Hand out the Class Code (shared) + each Access Code (private).
  4. Students sign in once — with those two codes, on any device, and use every lab.
  5. Watch progress — your dashboard shows who finished what and their Test/Quiz scores, updating live.

4What counts (and what doesn't)

Recorded: completed lessons and final Test / Quiz results (a student may retake — the best result is kept).

Not graded: the Learn and Practice steps, mini-games, and vocabulary — students explore those freely with no pressure.

Fingering is shown as guidance only — it's a recommendation, never something the app checks.
